### Understanding Prompt Structure:

Most effective prompts, like the examples below, often incorporate several key elements:

- **Subject & Motion:** Clearly define what the main subject is and how it moves or appears.
- **Lighting & Atmosphere:** Describe specific lighting conditions, time of day, and mood.
- **Composition & Framing:** Specify camera angle, distance, and aspect ratio.
- **Color & Texture:** Detail color palettes, surface textures, and material properties.
- **Style & Mood:** Indicate the desired aesthetic and emotional quality.
- **Technical Parameters:** Include chaos values, style settings, and profiles.

### Tips for Success:

- **Layer Details:** Build prompts with multiple layers of description for depth.
- **Use Motion Strategically:** Motion blur can add dynamism and emotion to static subjects.
- **Control Chaos:** Lower chaos values (4-25) for more predictable results, higher for variety.
- **Profile Selection:** Different profiles dramatically change the output style.
- **Aspect Ratios:** 9:16 works excellently for mobile and portrait compositions.
