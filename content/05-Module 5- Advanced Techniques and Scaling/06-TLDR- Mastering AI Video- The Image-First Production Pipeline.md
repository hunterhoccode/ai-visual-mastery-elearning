# The State of AI Video: A Practical Workflow for Mid-2025

## 1. Text-to-Video Isn't There Yet

Full text-to-video generation, while promising, often falls short of professional expectations. The results can be slow to generate, contain visual glitches, and frequently lack the fine-tuned style control needed for high-quality work.

## 2. The Better Workflow: Image First, Video Second

For greater visual consistency and creative control, the most effective workflow is to first generate a refined still image using a detailed Alpha Prompt. Once the aesthetic, composition, and lighting are locked in, that image can then be animated. This approach ensures the final video aligns perfectly with your intended style.

## 3. Keep Your Motion Prompt Short and Clear

Complex descriptions are unnecessary for animating an image. A simple, direct motion prompt is most effective. For example:

"Camera pans slowly left to right, soft directional light from above."

This provides clear guidance to the video model without overcomplicating the instructions and introducing potential errors.

## 4. What Works Well

Current AI video models excel at generating subtle and controlled movements, including:

- **Close-up portraits:** These are consistently the most reliable and highest-quality outputs.
- **Simple motion:** Blinking eyes, a slow head turn, or realistic breathing effects.
- **Atmospheric changes:** Shifting light and shadows.
- **Environmental effects:** Wind, fog, and simple camera movements like a slow push-in or shake.

## 5. What Doesn't Work Well

The technology still struggles with more complex and dynamic scenes. Common problem areas include:

- **Vehicles:** Cars and other vehicles often glitch, distort, or deform during movement.
- **Complex action:** Actions like breakdancing, flips, or intricate choreography are not yet rendered reliably.
- **Full-body tracking:** Long shots that require tracking a full body in motion remain a significant challenge.

## 6. Best Tools Right Now (Mid-2025)

- **Veo 3 (Google):** Delivers the highest visual quality and can generate longer clips, though generation times can be slow.
- **Runway Gen-3:** Known for its smooth camera motion and is excellent for fast iteration and testing ideas.
- **Sora (OpenAI):** Produces highly polished, cinematic outputs through a user-friendly chat-based interface, but clips are generally limited to the 10-20 second range.
- **LTX Studio:** The standout choice for animating existing still images and building out scenes with a cohesive flow.

## 7. Post-Process to Elevate the Result

The raw output from an AI video model is rarely the final product. Always enhance the video in a post-processing application to achieve a professional finish. Key steps include:

- Adding **grain** for a more organic, realistic texture.
- Applying **bloom** to create a cinematic, diffused glow on highlights.
- **Upscaling and color grading** to sharpen the image and ensure the tone matches your project's overall aesthetic.

Even if a video isn't perfect directly from the model, these finishing touches can polish the result and make it feel professional and intentional.
